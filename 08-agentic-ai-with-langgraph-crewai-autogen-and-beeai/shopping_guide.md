## Money-Saving Tips & Strategies

### 1. **Choose Dry Over Canned**
- **Savings:** 6x cheaper than canned chickpeas
- A 1 lb bag of dry chickpeas ($2.50) = 4-5 servings vs. 1 can ($1.29) = 1 serving
- **Annual savings:** ~$250 if using 2 cans/day equivalent

### 2. **Buy Store Brands**
- **Savings:** 20-30% off name brands
- Walmart Great Value, Kroger Simple Truth, Target Good & Gather
- Example: Store brand tahini $4.49 vs. name brand $6.99

### 3. **Purchase in Bulk**
- **Quinoa:** Costco 4.5 lb bag ($12-$14) = ~$0.50/serving vs. $1.00+/serving retail
- **Chickpeas:** 5 lb bag (~$6-$7) = ~$0.30/serving
- **Tahini:** Large jars (32 oz) often better value per ounce

### 4. **Shop Sales & Stock Up**
- Watch for olive oil sales (16-17 oz at $4-$5)
- Buy lemons in bags of 3-4 for better unit price
- Purchase kale when on sale and freeze portions

### 5. **Choose Conventional Produce**
- Conventional kale = half the price of organic
- Wash thoroughly with vinegar water solution
- Same nutritional value at 50% cost

### 6. **Maximize Pantry Staples**
- Tahini, olive oil, and spices last 8-10+ meals
- After initial purchase, future meals cost only $2.50-$3.50
- Build a well-stocked pantry to minimize recurring costs

### 7. **Unit Price Comparison**
- Always check price per ounce/pound, not package price
- Larger packages often have better unit prices
- Example: 16 oz tahini jar vs. 8 oz jar
