## Preparation Tips for Maximum Nutrition

1. **Soak Chickpeas Overnight:** Reduces phytic acid, improves mineral absorption, decreases cooking time
2. **Massage Kale:** Rub chopped kale with 1 teaspoon lemon juice and pinch of salt for 2-3 minutes to break down tough fibers and improve digestibility
3. **Toast Quinoa:** Lightly toast dry quinoa before cooking to enhance nutty flavor and nutrient bioavailability
4. **Activate Tahini:** Stir tahini thoroughly before use; separation is natural and stirring redistributes nutrients
5. **Add Dressing Last:** Apply tahini dressing just before serving to maintain kale's crisp texture and nutrient integrity
6. **Include Lemon Zest:** Grate lemon zest into dressing for additional flavonoids and vitamin C

## Potential Concerns & Solutions

### Concern 1: High Calorie Density
**Issue:** At 475-520 calories per serving, this may be high for some dietary goals
**Solution:** Implement Option C (Calorie-Conscious Version) to reduce to 400 calories while maintaining 24g protein

### Concern 2: Fat Content
**Issue:** 20-24g fat per serving is moderate-high
**Solution:** Fat is primarily from healthy sources (tahini, olive oil). If reducing, decrease olive oil first, maintain tahini for protein and minerals

### Concern 3: Sodium from Tahini
**Issue:** Some commercial tahini contains added salt
**Solution:** Choose unsalted tahini or reduce added sea salt in recipe

### Concern 4: Oxalates in Kale
**Issue:** Kale contains oxalates which may concern those with kidney stone history
**Solution:** Rotate with spinach, collard greens, or Swiss chard; ensure adequate calcium intake

### Concern 5: Phytic Acid in Legumes/Grains
**Issue:** May reduce mineral absorption
**Solution:** Soak chickpeas overnight, sprout if possible, pair with vitamin C-rich foods (lemon already included)
